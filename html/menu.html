Essential Coffee Guide

Photo Coffee Name Description Preparation Summary
ESPRESSO
 Espresso A single, rich, dark brown layer of concentrated coffee (~30ml/1 oz), with a think layer of reddish-brown crema. Served in a small espresso cup (demitasse). Brewed by forcing a small amount of nearly boilling water under pressure through finely-ground coffee beans. The crema forms naturally on top.
DOUBLE ESPRESSO
 Doppio A double shot of rich, dark brown coffee (~60ml/2oz), with a layer of reddish-brown crema. Served in a small espresso cup (demitasse) or slightly larger. Essentially two espresso shots brewed together, resulting in a larger and stronger coffee. Topped with crema.
HOT WATER
ESPRESSO
 Americano Espresso shot(s) diluted with hot water, resulting in a coffee strength similar to drip coffee, but with a different flavor profile. Served in a standard coffee mug(150-200ml/5-7oz). An espresso shot is brewed into a cup, and then hot water is added on top to dilute the coffee.
MILK FOAM
ESPRESSO
 Macchiato An espresso shot "stained" or "marked" with a small dollop of white milk foam in the center on top. Served in a small espresso cup (demitasse, 30~40ml/1-1.5oz). An espresso shot is brewed, and a small amount of milk foam is spooned onto the center of the crema.

ESPRESSO
 Cappuccino An espresso-based coffee drink traditionally prepared with equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and thick, airy milk foam. Served in a rounded, bowl-shaped cup (180ml/6oz). Optional dusting of cocoa powder on top. Consists of an espresso shot, followed by an equal ammount of stearned milk, and topped with an equal amount of airy milk foam. Cocoa powder may be dusted on top.

ESPRESSO
 Latte A coffee drink made with an espresso shot, a large volume of steamed milk, and a thin layer (around 1cm) of microfoam. Often features latte art. Served in a tall glass or large mug (240-300ml/8-10oz). An espresso shot is poured into a cup, followed by steamed milk, and toppedd with a thin layer of microfoam. Latte art is commonly created on the surface.

HOT CHOCOLATE
ESPRESSO
 Mocha A chocolate-flavored coffee drink. It typically includes chocolate syrup/powder, an espresso shot, steamed milk, and often topped with whipped cream and chocolate shavings/drizzle. Served in a mug or irish coffee glass (240ml/8oz). Chocolate syrup is placed at the bottom of the cup, followed by an espresso shot. Steamed milk is then added, and it's often topped with whiped cream and a chocolate drizzle.
ESPRESSO
ICE CREAM
 Affogato A simple Italian dessert/drink consisting of a scroop of vanilla gelato or ice cream "drowned" with a shot of hot espresso poured over it. Served in a small bowl or glass (120ml/4oz). Features a melting effect where espresso meets ice cream. A scoop of vanilla gelato or ice cream is placed in a bowl or glass, and a shot of hot espresso is poured directly over it.
STEAMED MILK
ESPRESSO
 Cortado An espresso shot "cut" with an equal part of steamed milk, with verry little to foam. Served in a small glass (rocks glass or similar, ~100-120ml/3.5-4oz). Emphasizes a smooth blend of coffee and milk. An espresso shot is combined with an equal amount of warm steamed milk. The milk is steamed to be smooth with little to no foam.
STEAMED MILK
ESPRESSO
 Flat White An espresso-based drink (often a double shot or double ristretto) with steamed milk and a very thin, glossy layer of microfoam seamlessly integrated. Served in a ceramic cup (160~180ml/5-6oz). Emphasizes a velvelty texture. An espresso shot (often double) is combined with steamed milk that has a thin, velvety layer of microfoam integrated throughout the milk. The foam is flat and not distinct.
CREAM
ESPRESSO
 CON PANNA A single or double shot of espresso topped with a generous dollop of firm, sweetened whipped cream. Served in a small espresso cup (demitasse). An espresso shot is brewed, and a cold, fresh whipped cream is carefully floated on top before serving.

ESPRESSO
 Irish Coffee A cocktail consisting of hot brewed coffee, Irish whiskey, and sugar, stirred and topped with a thick layer of lightly whipped cream floated on top (not mixed). Served in a footed glass mug (Irish coffee glass, 240ml/8oz). Hot coffee is mixed with Irish whiskey and sugar. Thick, lightly whipped cream is then carefully floated on top so it doesn't mix with the coffee.
